Certifying Cause of Death
Overview

The cause of death section, a facsimile of which is shown below, follows guidelines recommended by the World Health Organization. While both the content and formatting of the cause of death section can vary by jurisdiction, the cause of death section consists of two major parts:

  • the direct causes of death or sequence leading to death (Part I), which includes the underlying cause of death (lowest line used in Part I), and
  • other significant conditions contributing to death (Part II).

In addition to Parts I and II, there may be other questions regarding autopsy, tobacco use, pregnancy, and manner of death.



The cause of death section is designed to elicit the opinion of the medical certifier. Causes of death on the death certificate represent a medical opinion that might vary among individual physicians. Regardless, a properly completed cause of death section provides an etiologic explanation of the type, order, and association of events resulting in death.

In certifying the cause of death, only the diseases, abnormalities, or conditions believed to have contributed to death should be reported. The conditions present at the time of death may be causally related to each other, that is, one condition may lead to another, which in turn leads to a third condition, and so forth. Death may also result from the combined effect of two or more independent or completely unrelated conditions.
