About Us
The Mortality Statistics Branch is a part of the Division of Vital Statistics (DVS) at the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS), which is within the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. It is the mission of NCHS to provide accurate, relevant, and timely statistical information to inform policies with the goal of improving the health of the American people. DVS collaborates with the vital registration systems of various jurisdictions throughout the United States to collect, process, and analyze mortality data for the population, including geographic, demographic, and cause of death information. These data are used to determine the characteristics of those dying in the United States and to calculate mortality rates and life expectancies. For more information, please visit our webpage at http://www.cdc.gov/nchs/deaths.htm, query data at https://wonder.cdc.gov, or contact us at MortalityData@cdc.gov.
